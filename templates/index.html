About the ELX Index

The ELX (Earth Liquidity Index) is a composite macro indicator that measures global liquidity conditions in real-time. Built by EarthOne Research, it aggregates five critical macro drivers into a single, interpretable score ranging from -100 (extreme contraction) to +100 (full expansion).

The five drivers are: Central Bank Balance Sheets (Fed total assets), Real Interest Rates (10Y Treasury minus CPI), USD Strength (DXY index), Energy Prices (WTI Crude Oil), and Credit Spreads (High Yield OAS). Each input is z-score normalized against its 5-year rolling history and weighted according to its empirical impact on global liquidity.

ELX classifies macro conditions into five regimes: Deep Contraction, Contraction, Neutral, Expansion, and Full Expansion. Portfolio managers use these regime signals to calibrate risk exposure across equities, bonds, commodities, and digital assets.
